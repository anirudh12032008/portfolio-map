## Why this matters to me

I started learning piano when I was 10, and it slowly became one of the most grounding parts of my life.
When everything else feels noisy, playing helps me focus on one small thing at a time: rhythm, touch, and breath.

## My current routine

- **Warm-up (10 mins):** scales, arpeggios, hand independence.
- **Technique block (20 mins):** finger control, tempo stability, dynamics.
- **Repertoire block (20–30 mins):** one classical, one modern piece.
- **Play for fun (10 mins):** improvisation or melody-by-ear.

## Favorite pieces I come back to

1. River Flows in You — Yiruma
2. Nuvole Bianche — Ludovico Einaudi
3. Comptine d’un autre été — Yann Tiersen

## What piano taught me

Piano changed the way I approach difficult things.
Most progress is invisible day to day, but very obvious month to month.
That mindset translates directly to coding, research, and life.
