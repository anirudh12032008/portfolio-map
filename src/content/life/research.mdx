Was invited to mentor 8 different teams ( 5 students each ) in the Incognito Blueprints research Bootcamp. [Incognito Blueprints Research Bootcamp](https://www.incognitoblueprints.com/researchbootcamp)

My role started one month prior the bootcamp with group meetings with advisors and to create problem statements for the students to work on, and to create a roadmap for the students to follow during the bootcamp.

During the bootcamp, I used to have weekly meetings with each team to guide them through the research process, proofread their papers before submission, and to help them with any questions they had regarding the research process

I was involved with topics from health, space, computer science and both qualitative and quantitative research and I helped the students to frame their research questions, design their research methods, and to find and analyze their data, and to write their papers in a way that is clear and concise

I was also responsible for evaluating the assignment the students had to submit every week and to give them feedback on how they can improve their work and to help them to stay on track with their research goals, along with this I was also responsible to provide indivdual feedback to each student on their research progress.

During the overall bootcamp I also organized a game night for the teams to help them to bond with each other and to have some fun.

Once the bootcamp was over, I also co authored two papers with the students based on my contributions to their research and for the final project showcase submission and I am very happy to see that we got accepted in the JKLST ( Journal of Knowledge Learning and Scientific Thinking ) and we got published in the issue of the [journal](https://jklst.org/index.php/home/issue/view/11) which was really amazing for me and the students as well :)

Overall it was a really wonderful experience for me to be a part of this bootcamp and to mentor and work with such talented and passionate students and advisors [Saloni Verma](https://www.linkedin.com/in/vermasaloni/) and [Karan Dhingra](https://www.linkedin.com/in/dhingra-karan/) who has 8+ years of experience at Harvard Medical School, University of Ottawa, University of Waterloo & New York Genome Center

Mentored 8 teams (5 students each) through ideation, execution, and publishing across two cohorts, and co-authored two papers.
References: [Paper 1](https://jklst.org/index.php/home/article/view/252) and [Paper 2](https://jklst.org/index.php/home/article/view/234)
