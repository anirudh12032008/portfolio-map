## Why this matters to me
Since Covid lockdown started in 2020, I had alot of free time and I used to love eating food but I never knew how to cook and I was as always very curious about it and started to watch some videos on Youtube and tried to check the kitchen and see are the ingredients available to make something and then told my mother I wanted to learn to cook she first jokingly ignored me and continued her work

after trying to convince her for a few days she finally agreed but my terms were a little different if I want to learn something then I want to do it on my own only but my mother was like "no no I will teach you and I will be there with you" but I wanted to do my own efforts and learn by myself :/

trying so hard to convince she finally agreed and just stayed in the kitchen while I was trying to figure stuff in the kitchen, my first dish was "Pasta" and yeah It was BADDDDDDDDDDDDDDDDDDDDDD like really bad but I was very happy to make something so I still ate it quitely 
 

then I continued to make more stuff like pasta, sandwiches, noodles which I used to eat from outside and then my family also realised and let me cook for once in a week and they were also happy as I used to experiment with something new each time lol

i also started to make indian meals like dal, rice, roti, panner ki sabji, and my best dish biryani and now I feel indpendent enough to cook for myself and my family also lets me live alone when they go somewhere as they trust that I can cook for myself 

cooking is not just a hobby or skills it lets me take a break from everything and takes me to a new place where I create something and then eat it :D
