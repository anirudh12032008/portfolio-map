## Why this matters to me

So I did create a few hardware projects before but I really wanted to create something that I can use in my daily life and ofc after seeing all of my friends making their own macropads I wanted to try to make mine too

There were 2 possible ways for me to go for it
1. Make a pcb and get it manufactured and then solder the components on it
2. Make it on a perfboard and handwire manually everysingle connection and components and design evething on my own

I always try to do things on my own way and the way with which I can learn the most so I went for the second option and I started to design the circuit and the layout of the macropad on my own and then I started to handwire everything on a perfboard and it was a very time consuming process but it was also very fun and I learned a lot from it!

I faced alot of issues at first like the switches were not designed for perfboards so I had to cut the legs of the switches cut the perfboard and then solder the wires to the switches and then solder them to the main perfboard which was a little different from the usual way of soldering the switches on a pcb but I managed to do it :D


During the process of making the connections I also faced alot of issues like the connections were not working or the wires were not soldered properly and I had to troubleshoot a lot of times and check each connection and then finally after a lot of trial and error I was able to make all the connections properly! The GND and VCC connections were the most difficult ones for me as I had to make sure that they are properly connected to all the switches and the microcontroller and I had to check each connection multiple times to make sure that there are no loose connections or short circuits! ( yeah there were 5 short circuits :sob: :sob: :sob: :sob: :sob: )

Multimeter came to a lot of help during the troubleshooting process! I also tested each component separately first to make sure that they are working properly and then connected them together and tested the whole circuit and it was a very satisfying moment for me when I saw that everything is working properly and the macropad is functioning as expected!!!!

I also wanted to add another twist so I made it wireless using WiFi and added a battery with a charging circuit to make it portable and I can use it anywhere without worrying about the wires and the power supply! 

And to finish it off I also designed a 3D printed case for it and printed it using my 3D printer and it looks really cool and gives it a nice glitter finish!
